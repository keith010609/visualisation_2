World Suicide rate
World Suicide rate Map
Suicide rate published by the WHO
 Suicide is calculated by number of suicide deaths in a year, divided by
the population and multiplied by 100 000. High-income countries
have systems in place for collecting information on causes of death
in the population. Many low- and middle-income countries do not have
such systems, and the numbers of deaths from specific causes have to
be estimated from incomplete data.

💀Lowest Suicide rate
★ Barbados has the lowest suicide rate which is 0.4.
💀Summary
Overall South America shows a lower suicide rate, where countries
except Guyana, Bolivia and Uruguay have a suicide rate below 10.
The region with the highest suicide rate is Russia and its nearby
countries including Belarus and Kazakhstan, with suicide rates over 20.
Life expectancy
Life expectancy
 Life expectancy is a statistical measure of the average (see below) time an
organism is expected to live, based on the year of its birth, its current age,
and other demographic factors including gender. Life expectancy is calculated
using current age-specific mortality rates of the particular year of interest.
寿 Highest life expectancy
★ Hong Kong has the highest life expectancy which is 84.7. Their
leading longevity is the result of fewer diseases of poverty while
suppressing the diseases of affluence. A unique combination of
economic prosperity and low levels of smoking with development
contributed to this achievement.

Sex ratio
Sex ratio
Ratio of males for each female
 The chart represents the human sex ratio over a country or region, by
denoting the ratio of males for each female in a population. The sex
ratio at birth is not equal: in every country births are male-biased.
There are biological reasons why there are slightly more boys born
every year than girls. The ‘natural’ sex ratio at birth is around 105 boys
per 100 girls (ranging from around 103 to 107 boys).

Population growth
 The chart shows the population growth rate and its projections over the

👥Highest population growth rate
★ Oman has the highest population growth rate which is 9.13.
The annual growth rate in Oman has been extremely high in recent
years due to a huge influx of immigrants. There are so many, that
they nearly make up half of the modern population.
👥Lowest population growth rate
★ Latvia has the lowest population growth rate which is -1.6.
In the last three decades, Latvia has experienced a drastic decrease
in its population. First came the collapse of the USSR and a mass
emigration of Russians in the 1990s. Then, EU membership in 2004 led
to another drop in population through migration. After that, the recession
in 2009 and a resultant exodus of people.
